It is mid-2025. OpenAI is valued at over $300B with roughly $4B in annual revenue. Nvidia briefly crossed $3T in market cap. Hundreds of AI startups have raised billions on promises of transformative products. Enterprise AI spending is growing fast, yet measurable ROI remains elusive for most adopters outside of a few narrow use cases (code generation, customer support automation, document processing). Sovereign AI initiatives are multiplying globally. The last major technology investment wave — the dot-com bubble (1995-2001) — followed a strikingly similar pattern: explosive valuations, widespread adoption narratives, real underlying technology, and then a 78% NASDAQ collapse that wiped out trillions before the actual internet era began in earnest.

Question: Are we experiencing an AI investment bubble that will inevitably burst and cause significant economic damage, or are the underlying fundamentals of this AI wave genuinely different enough to justify current valuations and sustain long-term growth without a systemic crash?
