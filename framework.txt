Analyze using the following weighted framework:

1. Squad Quality & Depth (30%): Evaluate the starting XI quality, bench depth, and positional balance. Which squads can absorb injuries or suspensions across 7 matches without a significant drop in quality? Assess the goalkeeper, defensive line, midfield control, and attacking threat as distinct dimensions.

2. Tactical Identity & Coaching (25%): Assess the head coach's tournament experience and tactical flexibility. Which teams have a clear, proven system vs. reliance on individual brilliance? How does each contender adapt when the initial game plan fails — as it inevitably will in a knockout round?

3. Tournament Pedigree & Mental Resilience (25%): Evaluate historical World Cup performance under pressure. Which nations consistently perform in knockouts vs. which collapse in quarterfinals or semifinals? Factor in the expanded 48-team format: the path to the final is longer, which rewards consistency over peak performance in one match.

4. Form, Fitness & Competition Context (20%): Assess recent international results (2024-2026 window), key player ages and injury history entering the tournament, and the physical demands of the host continent's summer heat and travel distances across three host countries.

Hard constraints on analysis:
- Name specific players, not just nationalities — argue from concrete squad facts
- Acknowledge the role of luck and draw bracket explicitly, not just quality
- Do not default to "the best team doesn't always win" as an escape from making a prediction — commit to a winner
- Distinguish between "most likely winner" and "best team in the world" — they are not always the same
- The disruptor analyst should question whether the entire framing of "favorites" is meaningful in a 48-team tournament with expanded upsets
